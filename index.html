The XOR Crisis

Now comes a fascinating part of our story - the moment that almost killed neural network research, only to lead to its greatest breakthrough.

Continue

In 1969, Marvin Minsky and Seymour Papert published a book showing that perceptrons couldn't solve a seemingly simple problem: the XOR function. Let's see why this was such a big deal.

Consider these four points:

  • (0,0) → Output: 0
  • (0,1) → Output: 1
  • (1,0) → Output: 1
  • (1,1) → Output: 0
Continue

Go ahead - try to draw a single straight line that correctly separates the 1s from the 0s. Impossible, right? This is what we mean by 'not linearly separable'.

This limitation isn't just about XOR - it means a single perceptron can't learn any pattern that requires a more complex boundary than a straight line. It's like trying to separate oil and water with a straight piece of cardboard - sometimes you need something more flexible!

Why It Matters

Understanding the XOR problem is crucial because it led to the development of multi-layer networks, which we'll explore in future lessons. Sometimes, limitations drive the greatest innovations!
